What is accessibility?

In its narrowest definition, accessibility is the design of products, devices, services, vehicles, or environments so as to be usable by people with disabilities. That is a good enough definition for now.

In the niche of Web Accessibility, we have quite some details to think about. In this webpage, I’ll walk you through some most basic elements of web accessibility.

Why is web accessibility so important?

According to the World Report on Disability provided by WHO, there are 1 billion people worldwide have disability related issues and over 61 million adults in the United States are currently living under disabilities.

Actually, by making your website accessible, you are ensuring that all of your potential users, not only the people with disabilities, have a decent user experience and are able to easily access your information.

By implementing accessibility best practices, you are also improving the usability of the site for all users. (usability.gov)

Keyboard Navigation

Keyboard navigation is designed for people who are unable to use a mouse or other pointing devices, or who want to use a keyboard whenever possible.

By pressing down the Tab key, you will be navigated to the next element on the website. By pressing down the key combination of Tab + Shift, you will be navigate to the previous element on the website.

Color Contrast Ratio

For people who are colorblind or weak, the color and contrast of a website will determine whether or not they can see content on the website.

Therefore, reasonable adjustment of color contrast is very important. The recommended color contrast ratio by WCAG 2.1, level AAA standard, is above 7.0 for small text and above 4.5 for large text.

You can click the button below and experience some high contrast color combinations, or find out the color contrast ratio of your own combination at contrast-ratio website.

Subtitles

Captions (called “subtitles” in some areas) provide content to people who are Deaf and hard-of-hearing.

Captions are a text version of the speech and non-speech audio information needed to understand the content.

They are synchronized with the audio and usually shown in a media player when users turn them on.(w3.org)

You can click the button below to enable built-in subtitles.

Font Size

The size and density of fonts can be adjusted freely, and different size gears can be set according to different target users.

They are synchronized with the audio and usually shown in a media player when users turn them on.(w3.org)
